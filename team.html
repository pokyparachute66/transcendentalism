Free Thought
Thoughts should be formed without any controlling factors
How is Self-Reliance represented?
Free thought is an important characteristic of transcendentalism. Free thought is the ability to think and express whatever you would like. Free thought should be as it seems, free. Some people do not support this claim. Governments and people around the world believe that free thought should be regulated and restrictions should be put in place. Trancendentalists do not share this belief. Though stories such as Civil Disobedience, it is shown that free thought is an extremely important right and a key feature to Transcendentalism.
Examples in Today's World
There are many different examples of free thought in today's world. Two of the biggest examples are through protest and social media. Protest allow a person to express their thoughts and fight for what they believe in. By attending a protest, you are using the transcendentalist feature of free thought and exercising your right to use it. You are also able to express free thoughts through social media. Social media is a ginormous platform in today's world with millions of users. Through social media, you are able to use free thought to express your opinions and beliefs with very little to no restrictions.
Comparison of Literature.
Transcendentalist literature that express the feature of free thought are works such as Civil Disobedience. In Civil Disobedience, Henry David Thoreau is expressing his displeasure with the government's position on slavery and attempts to engage in war with Mexico. The letter he writes is a clear example of free thought and shows that free thought is an important feature of transcendentalism. We see many different types of stories and articles today expressing free thought. An prime example are political articles. Politics are a highly debated topic and due to this, people write many articles and stories fighting for why their opinion is right. There articles are examples of free thought and allow people to express their thoughts and beliefs on specific topics.
